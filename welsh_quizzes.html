Gaelic is a language primarily spoke in Scotland, particularly the Outer Hebridies, and shares close ties with the other Goidelic languages, Irish and Manx. More distantly, all of these are part of the Celtic language family together with other languages like Welsh, Cornish and Breton. Though Gaelic was once the lingua franca of Scotland, increasing anglicization and prohibition of the language greatly decreased the language's use and nearly drove it to extinction. Despite efforts to eliminate the language, there are still 50,000 or so native speakers and an increading number of learners as the language was finally recognised in 2005. The influence of Gaelic is still obvious all over Scotland, in place names, given names, and local dialect.
